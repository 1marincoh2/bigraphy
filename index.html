Time Line

Date Achievement
october 1810
Babbage arrived at Trinity College (Cambridge), having been self-taught in contemporary mathematics (from reading Robert Woodhouse, Joseph-Louis Lagrange and Maria Gaetana Agnesi). He consequently was very disappointed with the way mathematics was taught at the university.
In 1812
Along with John Herschel, George Peacock and other friends, they formed the Analytical Society. At the same time Babbage was a member of other associations such as The Ghost Club, an association for the investigation of paranormal phenomena and The Extractors Club, focused on the release of members of the asylum, in case that it ever happened.
In 1822
in a letter to Sir Humphry Davy on the application of machinery to the calculation and printing of mathematical tables, he discussed the principles of a calculating machine. He also suggested a computer plan.

1816:The analytical engine was the first computer, it was created by Charles Babbage. The British government supported him to create the project of a difference machine, a mechanical device to perform repeated additions. Features of this machine include a memory that can store up to 1000 numbers
